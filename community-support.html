Practical, social and wellbeing support — no personal care

Our Community Support service helps people stay independent, connected and confident at home. It's flexible, local and built around what matters to each person. This service is unregulated, meaning it does not include personal care — and that's intentional. It allows us to offer longer, unrushed visits focused on wellbeing, routine and quality of life.

What we support with

  • Companionship and conversation
  • Domestic tasks (cleaning, tidying, laundry)
  • Shopping and errands
  • Meal preparation (no feeding)
  • Routines, prompts and wellbeing support
  • Walks, hobbies and community activities
  • Post‑discharge settling‑in support

Who this service is for

People who don't need personal care, but do want a bit of extra help to stay organised, connected and well — including older adults, people with low‑level mental health needs, and families needing flexible support.

What Community Support does not include

  • Washing, dressing or toileting
  • Medication administration
  • Helping someone stand, transfer or move
  • Continence support
  • Any task that should be delivered as regulated care

If needs change

If someone begins to need personal care, medication support or mobility assistance, we reassess and move them safely into our Regulated Care service. No one is left without the right kind of support.
