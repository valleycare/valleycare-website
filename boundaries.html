Clear Boundaries Protect Everyone

We run two services side‑by‑side: Regulated Care and Community Support. Keeping clear boundaries between them keeps clients safe, protects staff, and makes sure we stay compliant with CQC.

What Community Support does

  • Companionship and conversation
  • Domestic tasks (cleaning, tidying, laundry)
  • Shopping and errands
  • Meal preparation (no feeding)
  • Routines and wellbeing
  • Community access and appointments
  • Post‑discharge settling‑in

What Community Support does not include

  • Washing or dressing
  • Medication administration
  • Helping someone stand, transfer, or move
  • Continence support
  • Any task that should be delivered as regulated care

What Regulated Care does not include

  • Unplanned domestic tasks outside the care plan
  • "Doing a bit extra" that hasn't been agreed and risk‑assessed

If Needs Change

If someone begins to need personal care, medication support, or mobility assistance, we reassess and move them safely into our Regulated Care service. No one is left without the right kind of support.
